Welcome!

I'm a Tenure-Track Professor of Machine Intelligence at UTN. Read more about my bio here.

My research focuses on embodied spatio-temporal AI for human-centric robot autonomy. This includes research on scene representations and abstraction, on detection, prediction, and understanding of moving and changing entities, active perception and information gathering, as well as lifelong learning for continuous adaptation to the robot environment, embodiment, task, and human preference.

To learn more about ongoing research, projects, and publications, please visit the UTN Machine Intelligence Group website.
